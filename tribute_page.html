Kalpana Chawla

First Indian Woman in Space

Biography

Kalpana Chawla was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, India. Inspired by her fascination with flying, she pursued a career in aerospace engineering and eventually became an astronaut. Her journey from a small town in India to NASA remains an inspiration to millions.

Education

She earned her Bachelor of Engineering degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Punjab Engineering College, India. Later, she moved to the United States for higher studies, completing a Master of Science degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington, followed by a Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Colorado Boulder.

Career & NASA Missions

Kalpana Chawla joined NASA in 1994 and became an astronaut in 1997. Her first mission was aboard Space Shuttle Columbia (STS-87), where she worked as a mission specialist. Her second and final mission, STS-107, ended in tragedy on February 1, 2003, when the shuttle disintegrated during re-entry.

Achievements & Honors

First woman of Indian origin to travel to space.

Awarded the NASA Space Flight Medal posthumously.

Received the Congressional Space Medal of Honor posthumously.

Several universities, institutions, and scholarships are named in her honor.
